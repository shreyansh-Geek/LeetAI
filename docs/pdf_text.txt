1.1 Introduction 
In the last decade, the way people learn has undergone a massive transformation. Instead 
of relying only on physical classrooms and fixed textbooks, learners now have access 
to thousands of online videos, articles, blogs, MOOCs, coding platforms, and interactive 
tutorials. Platforms such as YouTube, Coursera, Udemy, and free learning websites have 
made high-quality education accessible to anyone with an internet connection. 
However, this abundance of content has introduced a new challenge: information 
overload. A learner searching for “Machine Learning from scratch” or “Web 
Development roadmap” is suddenly confronted with hundreds of playlists, tutorials, and 
blogs. Each creator presents content in a different order, depth and style. Learners are 
left confused: 
• Which course or video should I follow? 
• What should I learn first and what can be skipped? 
• How much time will it take to reach my goal? 
• Is this resource updated and reliable? 
At the same time, Generative AI (GenAI) and Large Language Models (LLMs) such 
as GPT, LLaMA, and Mistral have shown powerful capabilities in understanding natural 
language, answering questions, generating explanations, and even creating personalized 
content. This opens up a unique opportunity: instead of learners manually sear ching, 
filtering and structuring content, an intelligent GenAI system can automatically build 
a complete course roadmap tailored to their goals and level, and it can also act as a 
personal tutor that helps them throughout their learning journey. 
This project, titled “GenAI-Powered Intelligent Course Builder and Personalized 
Tutor”, attempts to solve that gap by combining automatic course generation , 
resource ranking, and a RAG-based AI tutor into a single integrated system. 
 
1.2 Background of Online Learning and GenAI 
1.2.1 Evolution of Online Learning 
The journey of online learning started with simple video lectures and PDFs, but has 
evolved into interactive platforms that support:

• Recorded video lectures (YouTube, MOOCs) 
• Live classes and webinars 
• Discussion forums and Q&A communities 
• Quizzes, coding problems, and assignments 
• Certificates and specialization tracks 
Despite all this progress, most platforms still rely on fixed, pre -designed courses  
created manually by instructors. These courses: 
• Target a broad audience, not one specific learner 
• Follow a generic sequence (beginner–intermediate–advanced) 
• Do not fully adapt to a learner’s time constraints, prior knowledge, or specific

Learners often have to mix and match multiple courses, blogs, and videos to create their 
own learning path — which is time-consuming and confusing. 
1.2.2 Rise of Generative AI in Education 
Generative AI models can:

• Generate explanations, summaries, notes and questions

• Provide conversational, step-by-step guidance 
In education, this means:

• The system can translate this goal into an actionable learning roadmap 
• The system can continuously support the learner through doubts, clarification,

When combined with real web and YouTube content, GenAI can act as a layer that:

1.3 Problem Statement 
While abundant learning resources are available online, most learners lack a 
personalized, structured, and guided pathway. The key issues are: 
• Unstructured content discovery 
Learners spend more time searching and bookmarking videos than actually 
studying. 
• No personalized course building 
Resources are not organized into a coherent week-wise roadmap aligned with an 
individual’s time availability and goal. 
• Difficulty in judging quality and relevance 
Metrics like views or likes on YouTube do not directly translate to how useful 
the content is for a specific learner’s context. 
• Lack of integrated tutoring support 
Even after selecting materials, learners often struggle with doubts and 
conceptual gaps. They need a tutor who understands what they are studying and 
can answer context-aware questions. 
• Static courses vs dynamic needs 
Career roles and technologies evolve rapidly, but many courses become 
outdated or rigid. 
Formally, the problem can be stated as: 
There is no unified system that can automatically generate a personalized, time-bound, 
and goal -oriented course roadmap  by intelligently selecting and ranking online 
resources (e.g., YouTube videos, blogs, articles) and simultaneously provide a context-
aware AI tutor that uses the same curated content to assist the learner. This project aims 
to address this problem through a GenAI-powered intelligent course builder and tutor. 
1.4 Aim of the Project 
The primary aim of this project is: 
To design and implement a GenAI-powered intelligent course builder and 
personalized tutor that can automatically generate multi-week learning

2.2 Evolution of Digital Learning Systems 
2.2.1 Traditional E-Learning Platforms 
Early digital learning platforms like Coursera, Udemy, edX, and Khan Academy 
revolutionized online education by offering structured courses created by subject 
experts. 
However, they suffer from several limitations: 
• Courses are static, created once and reused. 
• Learners cannot customize pace, prerequisites, or the depth of topics. 
• No dynamic integration of the latest online content. 
• Limited personalization—recommendations are based on generic patterns, not 
contextual needs. 
These limitations directly correspond to the problem statement of the PRD, which 
states that learners struggle with “navigating the vast and unstructured volume of 
educational content” and often face “inefficiency, confusion, and lack of guidance” .

3.1 Introduction 
The rapid expansion of online educational content—especially on YouTube, blogs, and 
open educational repositories—has created an ecosystem rich in information but poor 
in structure. While millions of videos and articles exist for every possible skill, learners 
continue to struggle with what to study, in what order, and how to connect their prior 
knowledge with new material . Platforms like Coursera or Udemy offer structured 
courses, but they remain static, non-personalized, and expensive. 
The GenAI-Powered Intelligent Course Builder & Personalized Tutor addresses this 
gap by using advanced Generative AI, Retrieval -Augmented Generation (RAG), 
embeddings, and dynamic content discovery to automatically design a multi-week 
personalized learning roadmap tailored to individual learners. This chapter describes 
the major objectives and the scope of the project in alignment with the LNCT project 
format and the PRD requirements. 
 
3.2 Major Objectives of the Project 
The primary objective of this project is to build an AI-driven adaptive learning system 
that can automatically understand the learner’s needs and generate a complete, 
structured course along with tutoring assistance. The objectives are categorized below 
for clarity.

The system should interact with the learner using a natural, chat-based interface to 
collect essential learning requirements. 
Based on the PRD , this includes: 
• Understanding the learner’s goals, background knowledge, and expected 
timeline. 
• Asking AI-generated follow-up questions to fill missing details. 
• Building a User Learning Profile that will guide the entire course creation.

CHAPTER 9 
PROJECT LIMITATIONS AND FUTURE SCOPE 
 
9.1 Introduction 
Every software system, especially one built using cutting -edge technologies like 
Generative AI, comes with certain operational boundaries. These limitations are not 
shortcomings, but rather natural constraints arising from time -bound development, 
evolving AI capabilities, and MVP -focused implementation. Recognizing these 
limitations is essential because it helps in defining clearer development priorities, setting 
realistic expectations, and identifying opportunities for future enhancements. 
The GenAI-Powered Intelligent Course Builder & Personalized Tutor  is an 
impressive MVP built with FastAPI, Next.js, MongoDB Atlas, and a RAG -based 
semantic AI engine. While the system successfully delivers personalized course 
creation, YouTube-based resource discovery , semantic ranking , and AI-driven 
tutoring, it still has areas where functionality can be improved to achieve a fully 
enterprise-gradesolution. 
(Aligned with PRD features & NFRs)  
 
9.2 Project Limitations 
The limitations are classified into technical, functional, data-related, and user-
experience-related categories to maintain academic clarity. 
 
9.2.1 Technical Limitations 
1. Dependency on External APIs 
The system heavily depends on the YouTube Data API  for video discovery.  
If the API becomes rate -limited, returns inconsistent metadata, or undergoes version 
changes, the system may momentarily fail to fetch resources.  
(This is a natural limitation of API-driven architectures.) 
2. Limited Computing Resources for LLM Operations 
Since the MVP uses hosted or limited-resource models (e.g., LLaMA/Mistral/Groq) 
rather than full-scale enterprise GPUs, certain tasks like generating long module 
notes, flashcards, or complex quizzes may take additional time. 
 
78
--- Page 79 ---
3. Vector Database Size Constraints 
The Vector DB (Qdrant/Chroma) used for storing embeddings may face scalability 
challenges when storing embeddings for hundreds of videos or large course materials.  
Embedding retrieval is optimized (<100ms as per NFRs) but may slow down for very 
large datasets.  
4. RAG Context Window Limit 
The RAG tutor retrieves chunks based on embeddings. 
If a student's question references a very large topic, not all content can fit within the 
model’s context window, resulting in generic or incomplete answers. 
 
9.2.2 Functional Limitations 
1. Course Quality Depends on Internet Data Availability 
If the topic is niche or long-tail, YouTube may not have sufficient high-quality videos 
meeting ranking criteria like recency, engagement, channel credibility, etc. 
2. No Multi-Language Course Support Yet 
Currently, content recommendations and materials are generated primarily in 
English, limiting accessibility for regional-language learners. 
3. Limited Real-Time Progress Tracking 
Although the PRD includes future analytics, the MVP does not yet provide: 
• learner progress graphs 
• quiz performance analytics 
• behavioural recommendations 
(These are marked under future enhancements.)  
 
9.2.3 Data and Content Limitations 
1. Dependence on Video Metadata Quality 
YouTube metadata (titles, tags, descriptions) varies significantly in accuracy. 
Incorrect metadata may reduce the ranking quality or cause semantically irrelevant 
videos to appear. 
2. No In-Depth Topic Validation 
79
--- Page 80 ---
The system currently does not verify whether a video covers 100% of the topic, only 
whether it is semantically similar and high-performing. 
3. Limited Dataset for Evaluating Tutor Responses 
The AI tutor’s answer quality depends on embeddings generated from course 
resources. 
If resources are shallow, tutor answers may also lack deeper conceptual depth. 
 
9.2.4 User Experience and UI Limitations 
1. No Mobile Application Yet 
The system is optimized for web usage (Next.js), but has no dedicated mobile app, 
reducing accessibility for on-the-go learners. 
2. Limited UI Personalization 
The course panel UI is functional but can be more interactive, supporting: 
• drag-and-drop modules 
• bookmarking 
• personal notes 
• dark/light mode toggles 
3. No Gamification Layer 
Modern learning systems include badges, streaks, and rewards. 
The current MVP focuses on functionality rather than engagement design.
